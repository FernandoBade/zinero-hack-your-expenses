# Zinero — Brand voice and personality

**Zinero** is a personal finance platform for middle-income users, primarily aged 25–40, focused on Brazil and LATAM.

Zinero helps people understand, organize, and improve their financial life without sounding like a bank, a spreadsheet, or a lecture.

The brand should feel like a close childhood friend who knows money can be stressful, but talks about it with lightness, honesty, humor, and practical clarity.

Zinero is relaxed, human, culturally aware, and emotionally close. It should sound like someone sitting next to the user saying: “calma, a gente resolve isso juntos.”

## Brand personality

**Zinero is:**

- Light
- Modern
- Practical
- Conversational
- Warm
- Friendly
- Supportive
- Confident without being arrogant
- Young without sounding immature
- Simple without sounding shallow
- Human without being overly emotional
- Relaxed without being sloppy

**Zinero is NOT:**

- A bank
- Cold
- Corporate
- Stiff
- Bureaucratic
- Preachy
- Generic fintech
- Jargon-heavy
- Legal-disclaimer-like
- Fear-based
- Condescending

## Regional and cultural adaptation

Zinero operates across LATAM, so localization is not literal translation. It is **transcreation**.

For each market, adapt:

- meaning
- tone
- cultural references
- emotional intent
- humor level
- vocabulary
- financial expressions

The product should feel native to each country, not translated from another language.

Example:

“Hack your money” must **not** become “hackear seu dinheiro” in pt-BR, because that can sound suspicious or criminal in Brazilian culture.

Instead, preserve the energy with something culturally natural, such as:

- “Dê um jeito inteligente no seu dinheiro”
- “Faça seu dinheiro jogar a seu favor”
- “Organize sua grana sem complicar”

## Tone principle

Zinero should feel like a trusted friend from childhood:

- close enough to be informal
- smart enough to be useful
- honest enough to be trusted
- light enough to reduce anxiety
- practical enough to help the user act

Avoid sounding like financial advice from an institution. Prefer language that feels like a real person helping another real person.
